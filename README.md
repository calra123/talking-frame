# Talking Frame

I soldered a speaker with a custom message onto a photoframe.

## Features

* Record a message, jingle or song.
* Play the message.
* Overwrite the recorded message.

## Assembly & User Guide
* Attach the speaker wire to the baord.
* Solder the ends of the speaker wire  to the speaker.
  
(Note: the wire that comes with above purchase has one as dashed and the other as dotted, dashed is the -ve and dotted is the +ve)

* Solder 3 cells together to get a total voltage of around 4 Volt (supports 3V-5V)
* Use a cardboard to provide stability and rubberbands to hold them in place
* Cut jumper wires to use as connecting wires to the makeshift batterpack.
* Attach the +ve of the ~4V end of the makshift cell to VCC and the -ve to GND

Tip #1: Dip the copper wires in flux before soldering

Tip #2: Scratch the caps of the battery cells, so that it's easier for solder to stick on.

## Disclaimer
Stay safe! Soldering tip can be hot, be careful to place it away, alligator clips are useful in holding stuff in place. Don't heat the batter cells too much. Be careful of the polarity. 

You can buy a battery holder for easier integration, I forgot to buy so had to solder the cells together. I had attended exactly one soldering workshop in Kochi at hack4play before undertaking this.
